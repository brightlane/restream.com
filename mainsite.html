How Restream Works — The Technical Picture

Restream operates as a cloud multistreaming hub. When you set up your broadcast software — OBS, Streamlabs, XSplit, or even a hardware encoder like Magewell — you point your RTMP output to Restream's ingest servers instead of directly to YouTube or Twitch. Restream receives your single video and audio stream, then clones and distributes it to every destination you've added to your account.

This architecture delivers three critical advantages: First, your upload bandwidth requirements stay constant — streaming to 10 platforms costs you the same bandwidth as streaming to 1. Second, platform outages are isolated — if Twitch has a problem, your YouTube and Facebook streams keep running. Third, you get a centralized dashboard to monitor chat, analytics, and stream health across every platform in one place.

Restream also offers a browser-based studio — Restream Studio — which means you can go live without any software installation at all, directly from Chrome or Edge. This is particularly powerful for teams and enterprises who need streamlined workflows without IT overhead.

Getting Started

How to Start Multistreaming with Restream in 5 Minutes

1

Create Your Free Restream Account

Visit try.restream.io and sign up with your email or Google/Facebook account. No credit card required for the free plan. You'll be inside the dashboard in under 60 seconds.

2

Connect Your Streaming Destinations

Click "Add Channel" and select the platforms you want to stream to. For most major platforms like YouTube, Twitch, and Facebook, it's a simple OAuth connection — one click, done. For custom RTMP destinations, paste in your server URL and stream key.

3

Configure Your Streaming Software

Copy your Restream RTMP URL and stream key from the dashboard. In OBS Studio, go to Settings → Stream → Custom RTMP and paste them. Or use the Restream OBS plugin for even easier setup. Streamlabs and XSplit have built-in Restream integration.

4

Run a Test Stream

Before going live, use OBS's "Start Streaming" to send a 30-second test. Check the Restream dashboard to confirm all channels show green status. Watch for any channels showing errors — usually a token refresh or re-authorization fixes it.

5

Go Live and Monitor Unified Chat

Hit "Start Streaming" and watch your audience grow across every platform simultaneously. Open the Restream chat overlay in OBS or monitor it from the dashboard. Respond to comments, track viewer counts, and enjoy your expanded reach.
